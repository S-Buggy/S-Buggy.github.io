Poster

This piece of work was used as the identity design for the opera, Banished, held in Kilmainham Gaol on the 18th January 2019. I worked with the the Royal Irish Academy of Music, and produced a promotional poster and programme. The opera is about Irish female criminals who are banished out of the country on a ship in the 1800s. The poster is based around the feelings of claustrophobia and longing to escape that the women experienced onboard the ship.

The Brief
This piece of work was used as the identity design for the opera, Banished, held in Kilmainham Gaol on the 18th January 2019. I worked with the the Royal Irish Academy of Music, and produced a promotional poster and programme. The opera is about Irish female criminals who are banished out of the country on a ship in the 1800s. The poster is based around the feelings of claustrophobia and longing to escape that the women experienced onboard the ship.
The Visual Sparkle
The visual sparkle of this project is the striking image. The white space around the womans face instantly draws the viewers eye towards her and the tearing of the paper represents her longing to escape.

Promotional poster
Opera programme
The programme is also done in a classical, simplistic style and the occasional tear across pages brings the visual language from the poster into play.
